RESULTS

33 species found

From the families Accipitridae, Alcedinidae, Artamidae, Ardeidae, Charadriidae, Columbidae, Cisticolidae, Corvidae, Cuculidae, Estrildidae, Hirundinidae, Motacillidae, Nectariniidae, Passeridae, Phasianidae, Phylloscopidae, Pycnonotidae, Rallidae, Recurvirostridae, Scolopacidae, and Sturnidae.

Analysis

The results of the analysis with PAST for the Shannon Index are 2,384.

Conclusion

For educational purposes, rice fields can be a laboratory and bird watching practice, while as a tourism area it can become a birdwatching area.

Ecological Data

LC = Least Concern, EN = Endangered, VU = Vulnerable, NT = Near Threatened

Artamus leucoryn

(Linnaeus, 1771)

Indonesia, Philippines, Pacific, Australia (Invasive)

Small in size, forming large colonies, often seen in rice fields and grasslands, grain eaters.

LC

Passer montanus

(Linnaeus, 1758)

Europe, Asia, Southeast Asia (Invasive)

LC

(Linnaeus, 1766)

South Asia, Indonesia (Invasive)

EN

Lonchura oryzivora

(Linnaeus, 1758)

Java (native), all of Indonesia, East Asia and in many countries (Invasive)

This introduced bird is small in size, is a bird that forms colonies in quite large numbers and has a striking grey body color and a red beak, often seen in rice fields and grasslands, and eats seeds.

LC
